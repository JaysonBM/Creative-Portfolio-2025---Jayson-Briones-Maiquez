The Kingdom Brand

The Kingdom is not just a brand; It's a movement that encourages and inspires others to love and grow in knowledge and skill. The Kingdom helps others to continuously gain confidence through the teachings of the Word and help them be able to share their own experience in their style and language. The Kingdom is an expression of how good God is while being in the culture of Hip hop.

Process

Purpose

The Kingdom project endeavors to build a brand that seamlessly integrates the timeless message of God's word with the contemporary culture of hip hop. It seeks to overcome the challenge of sharing the Word of God while maintaining relevance and appeal to a younger audience.

Phase 2

Logo Design

The logo was made on Adobe Illustrator and my logo is to represent the "King of Kings" Which is to have a royal crown and a crown of thorns and combine those two so that the crown of thorns would be atop the royal crown.

Phase 3

Brand Guidelines

When building this brand we had to build a brand guideline which I've done on Adobe InDesign where I talked about the design elements of the brand which include: Colour, Typography, Mission, Vision, Moodboards, and Logo. The guideline was meant for the designer to make sense of the brand and develop that further. I referenced the given template that was used for this Brand Guideline but I did my best to design the template into my kind of design. During the process of developing colour for my brand I chose the colours that visually looked good and made sense of the brand in terms of colour theory. For typography I made sure it looked modern so I did a Sans-Serif font so that it would be legible and look appealing to everyone. I included the Mission and Vision of the Brand in this guideline to further explain the Brand's voice and their purpose of being a brand. The moodboards of this guideline were to give off the feeling of the brand give the reader the expectancy of who the brand is in images.

Phase 4

Brand Campaigns

Experiential campaigns that were about going out to a public area and do some portrait photography which would be immediately displayed to a screen that we would expect helps them gain confidence through the day.
